Helplines and Resources

If you have been a victim of digital fraud, it's important to report it. Here are some official government resources that can help.

United States

  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
    The FTC is the main agency for collecting reports of fraud, scams, and bad business practices. You can report fraud online through their website.
    ReportFraud.ftc.gov
  • Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)
    The IC3 is a partnership between the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) and the National White Collar Crime Center. It's the central hub for reporting cyber-enabled crime.
    www.ic3.gov

International Resources

Many countries have their own national fraud and cybercrime reporting centers. If you are outside the United States, we recommend searching online for the official fraud reporting agency in your country.

Common search terms you can use include:

  • "report online fraud [your country name]"
  • "cybercrime reporting agency [your country name]"
  • "national fraud helpline [your country name]"

Always make sure you are using an official government website before submitting any personal information.
