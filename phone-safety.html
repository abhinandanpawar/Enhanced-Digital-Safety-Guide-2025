Phone Safety

Our phones are powerful tools, but they can also be targets for fraudsters. Here's how to keep your mobile device secure.

Use a Strong Passcode or Biometrics

Protect your device with a strong, unique passcode, or use biometric features like fingerprint or face ID. This prevents unauthorized access to your phone if it's lost or stolen.

Be Mindful of App Permissions

When you install a new app, it will ask for permissions to access certain features of your phone, like your contacts or location. Only grant permissions that are necessary for the app to function. Be wary of apps that ask for more access than they need.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Transactions

Public Wi-Fi networks are often not secure. Avoid using them for banking, shopping, or other activities that involve sensitive information. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) to encrypt your connection.

Recognize Mobile Phishing (Smishing)

Smishing is like phishing, but it happens through text messages. Scammers will send you a text with a link, hoping you'll click it and reveal personal information. Be cautious of unexpected texts, especially those that create a sense of urgency or promise a reward.
