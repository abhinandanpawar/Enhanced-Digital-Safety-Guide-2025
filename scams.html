Common Scams

Fraudsters are constantly coming up with new ways to trick people. Here are some of the most common scams to watch out for.

Phishing Scams

As mentioned in the Digital Safety section, phishing scams involve emails, text messages, or websites that are designed to look like they're from a legitimate company. They aim to trick you into providing sensitive information like your password or credit card number.

How to avoid them: Never click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders. Always verify the sender's email address and look for spelling and grammar errors.

Tech Support Scams

In a tech support scam, a fraudster will contact you pretending to be from a well-known tech company like Microsoft or Apple. They'll claim that your computer has a virus and that you need to pay them to fix it. They may ask for remote access to your computer, which can allow them to install malware.

How to avoid them: Legitimate tech companies will not contact you unsolicited. If you get a call or a pop-up message about a computer problem, it's likely a scam. Don't give anyone remote access to your computer unless you have initiated the contact with a trusted company.

Fake E-commerce Sites

These are fraudulent websites that are set up to look like real online stores. They may offer products at very low prices to lure you in. If you make a purchase, you'll either receive a counterfeit item or nothing at all.

How to avoid them: Stick to well-known, reputable websites. Look for reviews of the seller before making a purchase. Be wary of deals that seem too good to be true.
