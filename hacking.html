How to Know If You've Been Hacked (And What to Do)

A computer hack is rarely as obvious as a big warning message on your screen. Most of the time, cyberattacks are subtle and can go undetected for a long time. Here are some common signs that your computer or accounts may have been compromised.

7 Signs Your Computer Has Been Hacked

  1. Applications Appear That You Didn’t Install: If you see new programs or apps on your device that you don't remember installing, it could be a sign of a breach.
  2. Unusual Disk Activity: If you see your mouse moving on its own, or your hard drive is constantly working even when you're not doing anything, it could be a sign that a hacker is controlling your device remotely.
  3. Strange Messages Sent From Your Accounts: If your friends or contacts tell you they've received strange emails or social media messages from you that you didn't send, your accounts may be compromised.
  4. Passwords Suddenly Don’t Work: If your login credentials for a website or service suddenly stop working, a hacker may have gained access and changed them.
  5. Unwanted Browser Toolbars and Pop-ups: If your browser has new toolbars you didn't install, or you're seeing a lot of pop-up ads, your browser may have been hijacked.
  6. Unwanted Software Installations: Many malicious programs bundle themselves with legitimate software. Always be careful what you agree to install.
  7. Strange Network Traffic Patterns: If you notice a lot of unexpected network activity, especially to or from countries you don't do business with, it could be a sign that your data is being stolen.

What to Do Immediately (Disaster Management)

  • Disconnect from the Internet: If you suspect a hack, immediately disconnect your computer from the internet to prevent the hacker from causing more damage.
  • Change Your Passwords: Change the passwords for all your important accounts, especially email, banking, and social media. Use a different, secure computer to do this if possible.
  • Run Antivirus and Anti-Malware Scans: Use a reputable security program to scan your computer for viruses and malware and remove any threats it finds.
  • Notify Your Contacts: Let your friends, family, and colleagues know that your accounts may have been compromised, so they can be wary of any strange messages from you.
  • Report the Incident: Report the hack to the relevant authorities, such as the FTC or the FBI's IC3.

How to Prevent Hacking

  • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: This is your first line of defense.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): This adds a crucial layer of security to your accounts.
  • Keep Your Software Updated: Software updates often contain important security patches.
  • Be Wary of Phishing: Don't click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders.
  • Use a Firewall: A firewall can help block unauthorized access to your computer.
